Donor Guidlines

Who is eligible to donate blood?

Basic Eligibility Requirements

  1. Age:
    • Usually 18 to 60 years old (some places allow 17 with parental consent).
  2. Weight:
    • Must weigh at least 50 kg (110 lbs).
  3. Health Condition:
    • Must be healthy — no fever, cold, infection, or major illness at the time of donation.
  4. Hemoglobin Level:
    • Should be at least 12.5 g/dL (checked before donation).
  5. Time Gap Between Donations:
    • Men: Every 3 months
    • Women: Every 4 months
  6. Sleep & Food:
    • Must have enough sleep (6+ hours) the night before.
    • Eat a light meal and drink water before donating.

You Cannot Donate Blood If You:

  • Have a cold, flu, or fever.
  • Had major surgery within the last 6 months.
  • Are pregnant or recently gave birth.
  • Have diabetes, heart disease, or high blood pressure (not controlled).
  • Have used drugs or alcohol recently.
  • Have Hepatitis, HIV, or any infectious disease.

What to do before and after donation?

Before Donation

  1. Get enough sleep — at least 6–8 hours the night before.
  2. Eat a healthy meal — don’t donate on an empty stomach.
  3. Drink plenty of water — stay well-hydrated before arriving.
  4. Avoid alcohol or smoking — for at least 24 hours before donating.
  5. Bring an ID card — and any medical information if needed.
  6. Wear comfortable clothes — especially sleeves that can be rolled up easily.
  7. Stay calm and relaxed — don’t stress; the process is safe and quick.

After Donation

  1. Rest for 10–15 minutes — sit or lie down after donating.
  2. Drink fluids — water or juice helps replace lost fluids.
  3. Eat a light snack — cookies or fruit are ideal.
  4. Avoid heavy exercise — rest your arm and body for the next 24 hours.
  5. Keep the bandage on — for a few hours to avoid bleeding.
  6. If you feel dizzy or weak — lie down and raise your legs slightly.
  7. Thank yourself! — you just helped save a life.

How long the process takes?

Step-by-Step Time Guide

  1. Registration (5–10 minutes)
    • Fill out a short form and show your ID.
  2. Health Check (10–15 minutes)
    • A quick medical screening: blood pressure, hemoglobin test, temperature, and questions about your health.
  3. Donation (8–10 minutes)
    • The actual time it takes for your blood to be collected (usually about 450 ml).
  4. Rest & Refreshment (10–15 minutes)
    • Sit, relax, and have some water or juice before leaving.

Total Time: Around 45–60 minutes

Actual blood draw: Only 8–10 minutes

What to do if any side effects occur?

Common, Mild Side Effects (Normal)

You might feel:

  • Lightheaded or dizzy
  • Tired or weak
  • Minor bleeding or bruising at the needle site

What to Do:

  1. Lie down or sit with your head between your knees until you feel better.
  2. Drink plenty of fluids — water or juice helps you recover faster.
  3. Eat something sweet or salty if you feel faint.
  4. Keep the bandage on for at least 3–4 hours.
  5. Avoid lifting heavy objects or doing intense exercise for 24 hours.
  6. Get proper rest — your body will replace the donated blood within 24–48 hours.

If You Notice Any Serious Symptoms

Such as:

  • Continuous bleeding
  • Swelling or severe pain at the needle site
  • Fever, nausea, or rash
  • Fainting that doesn’t go away

Then:

  1. Contact the donation center or your doctor immediately.
  2. Call emergency services if it feels serious.
